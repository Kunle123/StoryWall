# StoryWall perfect story rubric (internal)

Use for **product decisions**, **QA**, **curating featured examples**, and (eventually) **publish-readiness checks**. Score each category **1–5**. Total **max 30**.

| Band | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **27–30** | Flagship — homepage, marketing, benchmark |
| **24–26** | Very strong — safe to feature and promote |
| **20–23** | Promising — usually 1–2 dimensions need work |
| **16–19** | Usable, not a showcase |
| **Under 16** | Not publish-ready — rethink, not just polish |

**Targets:** A strong StoryWall **24+ / 30**. Flagship example **27+ / 30**.

**Recommended minimum to actively feature** (homepage, etc.):

- **4+** on: premise clarity, narrative arc, visual contribution, shareability  
- **24+** overall  

---

## 1. Premise clarity

**Question:** Can a new visitor understand what this story is about, and why it matters, almost immediately?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **1** | Confusing, vague, or too broad |
| **2** | Topic somewhat clear; point fuzzy |
| **3** | Understandable; not especially sharp or compelling |
| **4** | Clear, focused, easy to grasp quickly |
| **5** | Instantly understandable and intrinsically interesting |

**What “5” looks like:** strong title; obvious subject; clear reason to care; narrow enough to feel like a real story, not a theme.

**Test:** Could someone answer “what is this about?” in **one sentence** after seeing the first screen?

---

## 2. Narrative arc

**Question:** Does the story actually *unfold*, rather than just present information?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **1** | No real arc; disconnected facts |
| **2** | Some sequence; little momentum |
| **3** | Basic beginning–middle–end |
| **4** | Clear progression with meaningful turns |
| **5** | Compelling arc: setup, escalation, turning point, payoff |

**What “5” looks like:** beginning sets context; inciting event; development/escalation; at least one major shift; ending that lands.

**Test:** If you **remove chronology**, does the story get much weaker? If yes, that’s a good sign.

---

## 3. Beat selection and pacing

**Question:** Has the story chosen the right moments, in the right number, in the right order?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **1** | Bloated, repetitive, or missing key moments |
| **2** | Some important beats; pacing off |
| **3** | Mostly works; some drag or jump |
| **4** | Well edited and nicely paced |
| **5** | Every panel earns its place; nothing missing or wasteful |

**What “5” looks like:** no filler beats; no repeated idea in different wording; no big unexplained jumps; each panel advances the story.

**Test:** For every beat: **If I remove this, does the story get worse?** If not, cut it.

---

## 4. Visual contribution

**Question:** Do the images materially improve the story?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **1** | Generic, distracting, or unnecessary |
| **2** | Some help; mostly decorative |
| **3** | Support the story reasonably well |
| **4** | More vivid and easier to follow |
| **5** | Story feels substantially better because of imagery |

**What “5” looks like:** strong image–story match; consistent tone/style; variety without chaos; images clarify emotion, place, stakes, or change.

**Test:** Would the story lose power if images disappeared? If **yes**, visuals are doing their job.

---

## 5. Emotional or intellectual payoff

**Question:** Does the story leave the reader with something meaningful?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **1** | Flat; no real takeaway |
| **2** | Some information; little impact |
| **3** | Decent takeaway; not memorable |
| **4** | Clear and satisfying payoff |
| **5** | Strong “aha,” resonance, or lasting insight |

**What “5” looks like:** reader feels one of: *Now I get it* · *That was moving* · *I didn’t know that* · *That changed how I see this* · *That ending landed*.

**Test:** After reading, is there **a sentence someone would remember or repeat**?

---

## 6. Shareability

**Question:** Would a user feel **proud** to share it, and would another person want to pass it on?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **1** | Unlikely to share |
| **2** | Maybe useful; not socially compelling |
| **3** | Shareable for some audiences |
| **4** | Strong likelihood in the right audience |
| **5** | Highly shareable: useful, beautiful, surprising, or identity-signaling |

**What “5” looks like:** polished enough to send; quick to understand; sharer gets social value; “you should see this” impulse.

**Test:** Would a user send this to a friend **without apology or explanation**?

---

## Fast publish checklist

Before shipping or featuring, ask:

- [ ] Premise clear in **under ~5 seconds**?
- [ ] Story **builds** noticeably from beat to beat?
- [ ] Any **filler** panels?
- [ ] Visuals add **meaning**, not only decoration?
- [ ] Real **ending / payoff**?
- [ ] Someone would be **proud to share**?

If **two or more** are “no,” it’s probably not ready.

---

## Flagship bonus (optional)

For stories you **feature publicly**, score each **0 or 1** (max **4**):

| Criterion | Question |
|-----------|------------|
| **Topical relevance** | Subject people already care about? |
| **Distinctiveness** | More original than a standard timeline post? |
| **Mobile beauty** | Looks excellent on a phone? |
| **Conversion potential** | Makes a new visitor want to try StoryWall? |

**Homepage-worthy:** ideally **26+ / 30** and **≥3 / 4** bonus.

---

## Internal diagnostic labels

Useful shorthand instead of “bad”:

| Label | Typical pattern |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Not ready** | Below bar overall |
| **Clear but flat** | Good premise, weak arc |
| **Good story, weak visuals** | Arc works; images generic |
| **Beautiful but unclear** | Strong visuals; point fuzzy |
| **Strong and shareable** | Feature candidate |
| **Featured quality** | Meets flagship + bonus |

---

## Common failure patterns

| Pattern | What’s wrong |
|---------|----------------|
| Clear premise, weak arc | Topic good; reads like facts in order |
| Good arc, weak visuals | Narrative works; images generic |
| Strong visuals, weak clarity | Looks nice; point unclear |
| Useful but not shareable | Informative; not polished or alive |
| Too much story | Every beat included, not the *best* beats |

---

## Shortest operating version

Score **1–5** each:

1. **Clear** (premise)  
2. **Chronological** (arc)  
3. **Selective** (beats/pacing)  
4. **Visually meaningful**  
5. **Satisfying** (payoff)  
6. **Shareable**  

Same **30**-point total and bands as above.
